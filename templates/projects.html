Goodyear Driving Academy

In 2016 I worked on a project for Goodyear developing a virtually driving game that encouraged young drivers to avoid distractions and quizzed them on elements of the highway code. It was built in Angular.js and Three.js The project has since been retired but click here to learn more.

RoboArmadillo Robotics Kit

While at school I participated in the Student Robotics competition which aimed to get teenagers into open source robotics. I enjoyed this immensely but I didn't think their robotics kit went far enough with it's accessibility so I designed a cheaper and less complex alternative using Raspberry Pis, Arduinos and Python, the code can be found here and a simple demo.

Young Rewired State

Over the past few years I've been involved in Young Rewired State, a week long summer hackathon for Under 18s both as a participant and mentor. In my first two years I was a category winner and finalist. When I became too old to compete I took on the role of centre lead and mentor allowing me to focus on refining my skills as an educator.

Great British Public Toilet Map

Last Year I worked on the Great British Public Toilet Map a project which originally sought to use crowd sourcing to map and classify public toilets across Britain however is now being used world-wide. Specifically I was involved with creating an admin panel using Node.js and Koa for data mining the project in order to look at ways it could be improved in the future.
